**Llama Pie**  
**Solo Song**  
**By David Richins**  
**Tune: Don McLean’s *American Pie***  
*I wrote this song as a follow up to the Llama Song which had been a huge hit the summer before. This one never had the intensity of the original because it’s so long, but it gets plenty of requests.*

A long, long time ago  
I can still remember  
How that Llama used to make folks smile  
And I knew if I had my chance  
I could make that Llama dance  
Then maybe you’d be happy for a while  
But late that summer made me shiver  
With every death threat they delivered  
Someone cried for mama  
And I couldn’t do one more Llama  
And I can’t remember if I cried  
When they dragged me to the side  
But something touched me deep inside  
The day the Llama died…

*Chorus*  
So bye-bye Mr. Tibetan Monk  
Drove my Ford to the fjord   
Cause my Llama was drunk  
Them good old monks are burning incense and pie  
Singing “this’ll be the day that I fly\!  
This’ll be the day that I fly\!”

Do you know that there’s Llama Lore?  
Tales I hope you won’t ignore  
Let the Llama tell you so  
Did you know that the Llama Great  
Invented spam and called it fate  
And used it for his meals on the go  
Well I know a tale of two slabs of flesh  
He put together with glue and mesh  
Sent it up in late afternoon  
Man I did that groovy moon  
I was a lonely teenage Llama Man  
With a dark green robe and Soup-In-A-Can  
But I knew I was under ban   
The day the Llama died…
